# Editorial Workflow — Bartosz's Guide

## How to pick an article
1. Tuesday + Saturday mornings you'll get an "Editorial picks" issue in this repo (and a line in your morning brief iMessage).
2. Open the issue on GitHub mobile. Tick the checkbox next to your pick. Drop a 2-3 sentence comment with your angle. Add the `editorial-picked` label.
3. Within 15 minutes, a draft PR will appear. Review it on GitHub (you can edit files inline).
4. When happy: add the `editorial-approved` label on the issue. The PR auto-merges and deploys.
5. To edit instead: comment your changes and re-label `editorial-picked`. The draft regenerates.

## How to suggest your own article
1. Open a new issue titled `Editorial suggestion: <your topic>`.
2. Write your angle in the body (2-5 sentences).
3. Add labels `editorial` + `editorial-picked`.
4. Draft PR appears within 15 min — review as above.

## Fallback
If you skip an editorial issue for more than 48 hours, the bot auto-publishes the first candidate as a regular automated article (no byline) to keep the cadence going. No harm done.
